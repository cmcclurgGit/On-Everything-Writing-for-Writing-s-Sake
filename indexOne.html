On Everything: Writing for Writing's Sake

Language and Communication

One like a set of fireworks, the other like a persistent stream or gale. New language can bring emotion from a single neuron and carry through the wind. Close study of many languages shows the instinct to adapt and create language anew at every crossing of time. But, change in communication is taxing. Forms of communication--timeless oral storytelling, music, or literature--continuously morph content and method, but their place within culture, the world, and the learning experience of being human remains still.
